Frequently Asked Questions

ॐ

What are the visiting hours for the ashram?

The ashram is open to visitors from 6:00 AM to 8:00 PM daily. However, if you wish to participate in specific programs or meet with Swamiji, we recommend checking the schedule in advance or contacting us to make an appointment.

Can I stay at the ashram? What are the accommodation options?

Yes, the ashram offers accommodation for spiritual seekers who wish to immerse themselves in ashram life. We have simple, clean rooms available in dormitory style or private rooms. Accommodation is provided on a donation basis, but advance booking is required, especially during peak seasons and retreat periods.

What should I bring if I'm staying at the ashram?

We recommend bringing modest, comfortable clothing suitable for meditation and yoga, personal toiletries, any medications you require, a flashlight, alarm clock, and a water bottle. Bedding and towels are provided, but you may wish to bring your own meditation cushion if you have specific preferences.

Is there a dress code at the ashram?

We request all visitors to dress modestly out of respect for the spiritual environment. This means clothing that covers the shoulders and knees. Loose, comfortable clothing is recommended, particularly for meditation and yoga sessions. Traditional Indian attire like kurtas, salwar kameez, or sarees are welcome but not required.

What kind of food is served at the ashram?

The ashram serves simple, nutritious vegetarian meals prepared with love and mindfulness. Most of our produce comes from our organic garden. Meals are typically Indian vegetarian cuisine, with some international options available. If you have specific dietary restrictions, please inform us in advance, and we will do our best to accommodate your needs.
